Integrated Value of Influence

IVI is the first integrative method that captures all topological dimensions of a network. It synergizes six centrality measures while removing their inherent biases to identify the most influential nodes.

Local Centrality
Immediate neighborhood

Measures how well-connected a node is to its direct neighbors. IVI integrates Degree Centrality and ClusterRank to capture local influence while correcting for clustering bias.

Degree Centrality (DC): Count of direct connections
ClusterRank (CR): Degree adjusted by local clustering coefficient
Why both? High-degree nodes in dense clusters are less influential than those bridging clusters
🔗
Semi-Local Centrality
Second-order neighborhood

Captures influence beyond immediate neighbors. IVI integrates Neighborhood Connectivity and Local H-index to assess a node's power within its extended local environment.

Neighborhood Connectivity (NC): Average degree of neighbors
Local H-index (LH-index): H-index applied to second-order neighbors
Why both? Reveals nodes whose neighbors are themselves well-connected
🌎
Global Centrality
Whole-network position

Measures a node's position in the entire network topology. IVI integrates Betweenness Centrality and Collective Influence to capture global bridging and spreading power.

Betweenness Centrality (BC): Fraction of shortest paths passing through node
Collective Influence (CI): Product of reduced degree and neighbors' reduced degrees at distance d
Why both? Identifies bridges between communities and multi-step influencers
📊
The IVI Formula
Synergy + bias removal

IVI = Hubness Score × Spreading Score. Hubness combines DC and LH-index; Spreading combines CR, NC, BC, and CI. This multiplicative integration ensures both local power and global reach.

Hubness Score: f(DC, LH_index) → local sovereignty
Spreading Score: f(CR, NC, BC, CI) → information dissemination
Key insight: Multiplication (not addition) means a node must excel in BOTH dimensions to score highly
Validation: Outperformed 12 other methods across 10 real-world networks

🔄 How IVI Works
1
Network Input
Provide an igraph object (directed/undirected, weighted/unweighted)
2
Compute Six Centralities
DC, CR, NC, LH-index, BC, and CI are calculated for every node
3
Integrate via IVI Formula
Hubness Score × Spreading Score = IVI (range-normalized 0-100)
4
Rank & Visualize
Use cent_network.vis() to color and size nodes by IVI
